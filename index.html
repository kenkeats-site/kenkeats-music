Drifter's Hymns

The Rambler

I was lost down a ditch early, then a drifter came by and taught me the tambourine. I hitched rides out east to play the circus, but they wouldn’t have me. So I begged on main street for a while till a bad-time woman took me in and showed me what women could do. I picked up the guitar at her place and began rambling around, searching out souls that knew the beat of life—Blind Willie, the forgotten people. Late-night bars to sailors fresh off the wharf; bad sets earned growls: “You gotta know how to play your axe, boy.” And that was that.

Scrimped my way back home to the west. The songs found shape—sweet folk for solitary hours, telling stories central to the human heart. Echoes of the great ones: Dylan’s riddle, Young’s ache, Springsteen’s thunder—filtered through thought-provoking lyrics and melodies that twist like backroads. Four years of solo rounds through Perth streets and venues honed the blade. In 2013 I formed a band called Kenneth Rogers and the Lucid Dream to carry the fire further when the moment calls for it.

These are songs for the lost, the searching, the ones who still hear the pulse beneath the noise.
